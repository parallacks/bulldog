Making Sense of Your Genetic Story

DNA testing has revolutionized genealogy, offering powerful new ways to discover your roots. However, understanding the results can be complex. We specialize in interpreting:

  • Autosomal DNA (atDNA): Used for finding relatives across all your ancestral lines within the last 5-7 generations (e.g., AncestryDNA, 23andMe, MyHeritage DNA, FamilyTreeDNA Family Finder).
  • Y-DNA: Traces the direct paternal line (father to son) and helps with surname studies.
  • Mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA): Traces the direct maternal line (mother to child).
  • Ethnicity Estimates: Understanding what these percentages mean and their limitations.
  • Match Lists: Identifying how you connect to your genetic matches and prioritizing the most relevant ones.
  • Advanced Tools: Chromosome browsers, segment data, and third-party tools like GEDmatch.

Whether you're new to genetic genealogy or have results you can't decipher, we provide clarity and actionable research paths.

Our DNA Analysis Approach

We combine genetic expertise with traditional genealogical methods to maximize your DNA test results:

Results Review & Goal Setting

We assess your current DNA results, discuss your research objectives, and determine how DNA can help answer your specific questions.

Match Management & Clustering

We help organize your DNA matches, identify common ancestors using tools like the Leeds Method, and build genetic networks.

Triangulation & Segment Analysis

For complex cases, we use segment data to confirm ancestral lines and pinpoint common ancestors with specific matches.

Integration with Traditional Research

DNA evidence is most powerful when combined with documentary evidence. We link genetic connections to your paper trail.

Our goal is to transform your raw DNA data into tangible family history discoveries and help you overcome research obstacles.

DNA Success Stories

Identifying an Unknown Grandparent

Laura had taken a DNA test hoping to identify her biological maternal grandfather, as her mother was adopted and had very little information.

Our Solution:

By meticulously analyzing Laura's top DNA matches, building out their family trees, and identifying shared ancestral couples, we narrowed down potential candidates. Further targeted testing of a key match confirmed the identity of her grandfather's family.

Breaking a Colonial Brick Wall

Robert was stuck on his 4x great-grandfather in 1780s Virginia. Documentary evidence was scarce and conflicting.

Our Solution:

We utilized Y-DNA results from a direct male descendant and autosomal DNA matches from various cousins. By identifying shared segments and tracing the lineages of these matches, we were able to pinpoint the correct ancestral couple, breaking through a decades-old brick wall.
