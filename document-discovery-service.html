Document Discovery

Unearthing the records that tell your ancestors' stories. We find the vital records, census data, and historical documents that bring your family history to life.

Why is Document Discovery Crucial?

Historical documents are the building blocks of accurate genealogy. They provide the evidence needed to confirm family relationships, dates, locations, and other vital details. Our Document Discovery service focuses on finding these critical records, which can include:

  • Vital Records: Birth, marriage, and death certificates.
  • Census Records: National, state, and local population enumerations.
  • Immigration & Naturalization Records: Passenger lists, border crossings, citizenship papers.
  • Military Records: Draft cards, service records, pension files.
  • Land Records: Deeds, mortgages, property tax records.
  • Court & Probate Records: Wills, estate files, civil and criminal court cases.
  • Church Records: Baptismal, marriage, and burial registers.
  • Newspapers & Periodicals: Obituaries, news articles, social mentions.

Locating these documents can often solve long-standing mysteries and add incredible depth to your understanding of your ancestors' lives.

Document Discoveries in Action

The Missing Will

Eleanor knew her great-great-grandfather was a man of some property, but his will could not be found in county records, leaving a gap in understanding family land distribution.

Our Solution:

By searching adjacent county records and lesser-known state archival collections, we located the "lost" will, which detailed not only property but also named previously unknown children and their spouses, significantly expanding the family tree.

Pinpointing the Arrival

David's family knew their ancestor arrived from Italy in the early 20th century, but the exact date and ship were unknown, hindering further research in Italian records.

Our Solution:

Through meticulous searching of passenger lists, considering various name spellings and ports of entry, we identified the correct arrival record. This document provided his town of origin in Italy, opening the door to tracing his Italian lineage.
